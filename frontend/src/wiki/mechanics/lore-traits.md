"The natural gift of the universe, Lore Traits are a part of you, as much as your brain or your emotions are a part of you."

Lore Traits are intrinsic parts of all creatures with [[Lore Power]]. Lore Traits can grant esoteric and special abilities, increased proficiency in various skills, increased learning ability, or even direct increases in physical, mental, or magical abilities. Many people are very interested in diversifying their Lore Traits and having as many types as they can get. There are infinite combinations of Lore Traits and every individual will have their own unique "build," which is a colloquial term for a [[Loreseekers|Loreseeker's]] Lore Trait combination as well as the Lore Items that complement them.

Many Lore Traits have restrictions on when they can be used and some have downsides in exchange for more power.

## How do Lore Traits develop?
There are two ways. Lore Traits can develop naturally through the Universal Lore, which will analyze your history, traits, and desires to form a trait that fits the creature. Lore Traits can also be developed artificially through Lore Essence. A Lorekeeper can activate a Lore Essence on a person to generate a Lore Trait. It has a high chance of success that gets lower depending on the person's number of existing traits as well as the person's Lore progression.

## Lore Trait Types
There are different categories and subcategories of Traits
### Universal
These are the generic Lore Traits that typically just increase learning ability or direct proficiency in various skills, or increase attributes. They also typically have very general or generic names like Swordsman, Marksman, Strength, etc. Just because they're simple doesn't mean they're any less powerful or important, in fact Universal traits are usually the foundation of a build. Universal traits don't have negatives.
#### Comprehension Type
A subcategory of Universal Trait that enhances comprehension or learning ability of certain skillsets or knowledges.
#### Characteristic Type
A subcategory of Universal Trait that enhances a particular characteristic. This could be physical or mental faculties, or more esoteric things like charisma.
#### Attribute Type (Maybe)
A subcategory similar to Characteristic but specifically Lore Power attributes. 
### Special
Sometimes, these have drawbacks.
#### Leader
This Lore Traits allows you to recruit followers, usually through a soul contract, and grant them a special Lore Trait related to this one. Very rare type of trait. These are powerful when the person with the Trait is able to actually convince people to perform the soul contract, as it's a powerful binding vow that puts a lot of power into the Leader's hands. Soul contracts are voluntary, meaning no one can force another into one normally, however there have been cases of people forced to choose between the contract and death.
#### Lorekeeper
This Lore Trait allows you to handle and store Lore Essence long-term without side effects. 
#### Loresmith
This Lore Trait allows you to manipulate Lore Essence and its innate components to create items.
#### Lorecaster
This Lore Trait allows you to manipulate Lore Power. This is magic. It requires vivid imagination.
#### Innate
Some species or descendants of powerful Loreseekers will be born with Lore Traits. Usually, these are species specific or specific to the parent or ancestor of the descendant. For example, Bloodline traits would be passed down to a descendant, which would then be categorized as an innate trait. For special species of creatures, all members of the species would be born with the same innate trait.
#### [[Revision]]
This Lore Trait is an altered version of an existing trait.

Unique: These are highly tailored to the person who developed the trait. In other words, all unique traits are specific to a person's character, history, existing skills, and especially their desires and goals. Best example is Victor's Loreframe Engineer trait, which is very related to his desire. Another example is Parthas' [[He of Many Eyes]].

Location-based: Some Lore Traits are location-based, which means they have minimal or no effect outside of those locations.
